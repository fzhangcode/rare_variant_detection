Abstract: We report a targeted, cost-effective method to quantify rare single-nucleotide polymorphisms from pooled human genomic DNA using second-generation sequencing. We pooled DNA from 1,111 individuals and targeted four genes to identify rare germline variants. Our base-calling algorithm, SNPSeeker, derived from large deviation theory, detected single-nucleotide polymorphisms present at frequencies below the raw error rate of the sequencing platform.

Comments: This paper developed a rare allelic variant quantification algorithm which is derived from large deviation theory. The algorithm, SNPSeeker,can detect and quantify rare variants in large pooled samples using second-generation sequencing. This method greatly improved the specificity of SNP calling,while there were not enough data to determine the sensitivity of the method for detection of very rare SNPs(MAF=0.045%).Other discoveries were made in this paper, and detailed algorithm is available in the supplementary information.
